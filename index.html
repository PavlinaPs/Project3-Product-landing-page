About

I am happy that you are visiting my page and that you are interested in knitting.

Whatever reason brought you to it, knitting has many benefits. Many studies were conducted and many blogs written on this topic, these benefits I find the most interesting and appealing:

  • Learning how to knit allows you to experience the joy of instant gratification. Being able to produce something within a few hours that you can actually wear is hugely satisfying.
  • On the physical side, knitting is good for your brain. It improves hand eye coordination and by helping you relax, it also reduces the heart rate, lowers blood pressure and generally minimizes stress. This simple repetitive activity of knitting produces more of the “feel good” hormones, serotonin and dopamine.
  • Knitting teaches patience.
  • Knitting is a great way to connect people.
  • Gone are the days when a knitted Christmas gift meant a scratchy pair of socks or a reindeer themed Christmas sweater. People actually look forward to receiving a handmade angora headband or a multi-color scarf and appreciate that it was something you made especially for them.
  • Shopping for wool is a sensory delight! Start your own treasure chest.

Source: Forget the funny sweater: Here are 6 real benefits of knitting

Socks

Socks are not the easiest items to knit, but once you have finished your first pair, you never stop knitting another.

You can choose from two kinds of needles - double pointed or circular. I prefer double pointed, even though I have to watch the end stitches so they not fall off the needles, it is easier to distribute stitches evenly and keep track of their count.

There is a lot of inspiration and many good instructions for beginners or advanced knitters:

Hats

Hats are the best projects for beginners on circular or double pointed needles. You can easily learn and practice decreases. Simple hats don't need any other tricks. Maybe a pompom in the end.

I learned knitting hats from video tutorial below. It combines the use of circular needles and double pointed needles in one project. As the stitch count gets smaller, double pointed needles make the knitting easier.

Gloves

You can choose a level of difficulty when knitting gloves, because there are projects for every level.

For beginners would be suitable for example fingerless gloves, where you only need to knit the thumb (or not, it can even be only a hole for the thumb). It might seem useless, such gloves, but oh yes, they are useful. You can use your cell phone. I made one pair for myself, reading facebook while in car waiting for the train to pass.

For advanced knitters I would suggest mittens, with some kind of pattern either in stitches or color. I sure you can find myriads of free patterns online.

And even for experts, five finger glove with a pattern could be a challenge. Or you can leave the fingers unclosed and knit a flip cover for them.

I bought a book about gloves, I think it is great. Here is the link: "Knit Mitts: Your Hand-y Guide to Knitting Mittens & Gloves"
